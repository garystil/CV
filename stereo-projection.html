Step 1: Stereographic Projection

Gary James Stilwell

The multi-planet astrolabe series was launched in the MIT seminar EC.090 'Recreate Experiments from History' led by Elizabeth Cavicchi. Background on astrolabe construction was primarily drawn from James Morrison's book 'The Astrolabe' and Alistair Kwan's unpublished 'Manual for Astrolabe Construction and Use.' Astronomical data was sourced from NASA's online 'Planetary Fact Sheet.' Latitude was standardized at 42.3736Â° N (MIT!) for all planets.

The digital sketches were drawn using p5.js, an online JavaScript library developed by Lauren McCarthy based on the core principle of Processing as conceived by Media Lab alumni Ben Fry and Casey Reas - to make coding accessible for artists, designers, and educators. In tribute to Al-Zarqali's metalsmithing skills, the designs were lasered on titanium, copper, aluminum and glass sheets using an Epilog Fusion M2 laser in MIT's makerspace Metropolis.
